## Scope interpretation

The document establishes that a written CFATA/CEID approval exists for a broad
human-research protocol that expressly includes non-invasive sweat sampling and
spectroscopic analysis. It is scientifically relevant to the same type of
proof-of-concept work, but does not by itself establish that the completed
sessions fall within its approved scope.

The letter postdates the repository's 2024 acquisitions. It does not explicitly
state that the decision applies retrospectively to those completed sessions,
nor does it mention public repository deposition, release of exact acquisition
timestamps, open licensing, or unrestricted downstream reuse. The repository
therefore does not describe it as prospective approval for the 2024 collection
or as permission for open data sharing.

The letter also does not name this paper or SERS proof-of-concept and does not
state a protocol version, submission date, approval term or expiry, participant
count, consent condition, or data-management condition. Its wording alone
cannot resolve those details.

## Clarification still required

Before an unqualified release, the authors should obtain written clarification
from CFATA/CEID on whether `CFATA/CEID/002-2026` covers:

1. the previously completed 2024 proof-of-concept collections and analysis;
2. public deposition of pseudonymised spectra and exact timestamps; and
3. downstream reuse under any proposed data licence.

The journal editor should receive the same chronology: collection in 2024, the
manuscript/thesis statement that no formal prior approval had been obtained,
and the subsequent 2026 approval letter. The committee and journal, rather than
this repository, must determine the effect of the later approval.

The structured, identifier-minimised record is available in
`metadata/ethics_approval_record.csv`.

The signed scan should not be published without CFATA/CEID permission. If a
public copy is later required, an institution-approved version with signatures
appropriately redacted would be safer than redistributing the source scan.
